Barrett Honors Thesis - Creative Project

All Barrett students work under the guidance of a Professor to complete a thesis. This can be a creative project or a research based thesis. I am working with Professor Yezhou Yang and PhD student Zihuan Fang on a creative project of my own.

The Project

I came up with an application idea for handheld devices using the front facing camera which will solve a persisting problem with these devicesðŸ¤³. I found a problem in many peoples' lives that can be solved with computer vision. I believe that this project can be turned into a software product of value.

For this application, I am training a convolutional neural network (CNN) to analyze the video feed coming from the front camera. This is a challenging task because handheld devices have limited computational power and battery. My application needs to be extremely efficient so that the device battery is not drained.

Results

So far I have created a demo that works on my iOS devicesðŸ“±. I used coreML in Swift to deploy a CNN originally trained in keras. I'm very happy to have a working demo already.

The Defense

I will defend my thesis project in the middle of October. It is open to the public, and I will post the date and location here later.

What I'm Learning

  1. Convolutional Neural Nets (CNNs) - There are infinite architectures that one can use in their CNN model. I am getting an idea of what works and doesn't work with different architectures of CNNs.
  2. Image Data Augmentation - To train a robust CNN, one needs labeled image data. I learned how to do this with keras and I created images with varying brightness, rotation, and more.
  3. Swift for iOS - I have deployed my solution to my iPhone using Swift. I had to learn the language in order to implement my specific application, which was not always fun. Swift isn't the easiest to learn in my opinion, but thankfully I'm past the begginner stages.
  4. Image pre-processing - I'm practicing my pre-processing skills by implementing laplacian filters and other edge detection algorithms. This makes it easy for the computer to perform image analysis and detect objects easier.
